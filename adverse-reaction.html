ADR Assessment Guide

Causality Assessment
  • Definite: Clear temporal relationship, known reaction, improvement on withdrawal
  • Probable: Reasonable temporal relationship, unlikely to be attributed to disease/other drugs
  • Possible: Reasonable temporal relationship, could be attributed to disease/other drugs
  • Unlikely: Temporality makes causal relationship improbable
Common Post-Transplant ADRs
Medication Common Adverse Effects
Tacrolimus Nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity, hyperglycemia, hypertension
Cyclosporine Nephrotoxicity, hypertension, hirsutism, gingival hyperplasia
Mycophenolate GI disturbances, leukopenia, increased infection risk
Corticosteroids Weight gain, mood changes, osteoporosis, glucose intolerance
Sirolimus Hyperlipidemia, thrombocytopenia, delayed wound healing
Any serious adverse events must be reported to relevant regulatory authorities.

Management Options

When an adverse drug reaction is confirmed:

  1. Dose modification: Adjust dosage based on severity
  2. Drug substitution: Switch to alternative medication
  3. Symptomatic treatment: Manage symptoms without changing immunosuppression
  4. Monitoring: Increase frequency of relevant laboratory tests
  5. Patient education: Inform about signs to watch for
